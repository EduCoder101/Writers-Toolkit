Show, Don't Tell

Paint pictures with your words.

What does "Show, Don't Tell" mean?

When we write, it's tempting to just tell the reader what happened: "He was scared." "She was happy." But interesting writers do something different. They describe the action so the reader can picture it for themselves.

We call this building mind pictures. Instead of telling the reader how to feel, we give them enough detail to feel it on their own.

Compare these two versions:

Telling

Daniel saw the monster and was scared, so he ran away as fast as possible.

Showing

Daniel felt a chill down his spine as he saw the monster. The feeling was familiar. It was fear. He knew what he had to do. He nearly slipped as he began to run away as fast as he could.

The "telling" version gives us facts. The "showing" version slows down the moment so we can feel what Daniel feels.

Technique 1: Add sensory details

👁

What can the character see, hear, feel, smell, or taste? "A chill down his spine" is something we can feel. "Clouds of dust puffed up with each step" is something we can see. The five senses are your most powerful tool.

Telling

He walked inside.

Showing

He breathlessly walked slowly inside. Step by step he ventured in further, clouds of dust puffed up with each step.

Technique 2: Show thoughts and reactions

🧠

Instead of writing "he was scared", show us what fear looks like: trembling, freezing, a racing heart, shallow breaths. Instead of "it was old", show the character thinking about what they notice.

Telling

The stairs were old.

Showing

Creaking and sagging under his weight, Andrew knew the stairs must be very old.

Technique 3: Stretch the moment

⏲

A single second of action can become a whole paragraph. The more important the moment, the more you slow it down. Ask yourself: what happened just before? What happened during? What happened just after?

Telling

The door closed behind him.

Showing

He jumped as with a loud thud the door closed behind him. He froze. Fear surged through his body. He hadn't touched it. How had it closed?

See the full transformation:

Before (bare bones)

Andrew walked up the wooden stairs. The front door was huge, much bigger than a normal door. He opened it gently and walked inside. With a loud thud the door closed behind him. He hadn't touched it. Was he alone? He started trembling and wondered would he ever get out of here alive.

After (with detail)

After thinking about it for some time, Andrew walked up the wooden stairs. Creaking and sagging under his weight, Andrew knew the stairs must be very old. The front door was huge, much bigger than a normal door. Perhaps a giant used to live here? He'd heard rumours but had never believed them. Opening the door gently, he breathlessly walked slowly inside. Step by step he ventured in further, clouds of dust puffed up with each step. He jumped as with a loud thud the door closed behind him. He froze. Fear surged through his body. He hadn't touched it. How had it closed? Was he alone? He started trembling and wondered would he ever get out of here alive.
