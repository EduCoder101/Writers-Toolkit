Learning Intention

We are learning to identify and use adverbial phrases to add detail to our sentences by telling the reader when, where, how or why something happened.

Success Criteria
  • I can explain that an adverbial phrase is a group of words that gives more information about a verb.
  • I can identify adverbial phrases in a sentence and say whether they tell us when, where, how or why.
  • I can add adverbial phrases to simple sentences to make my writing more detailed and interesting.
  • I can write my own sentences that include adverbial phrases for different purposes.

Which sentence paints a clearer picture?

Version A
The dog barked.
Version B
The dog barked loudly in the middle of the night.

What extra information does Version B give us that Version A does not?

Definition

Adverbial Phrase

A group of words that tells us more about a verb by answering when, where, how or why something happened.

Remember: a phrase is a group of words, not just one word. "Quickly" is an adverb. "With great speed" is an adverbial phrase.

Adverbial phrases answer four questions

Each type adds different detail to your writing. Click to reveal each one.

When?
after dinner, during the storm, before sunrise
Where?
in the playground, behind the shed, under the bridge
How?
with a smile, in complete silence, at lightning speed
Why?
because she was tired, to impress the crowd, for good luck

Let's think it through together

She ran with great speed.
Step 1: Find the verb
What is the action word? Ran is the verb.
Step 2: Find the extra detail
What group of words tells us more about the running? "With great speed" gives us extra information.
Step 3: Ask the question
How did she run? With great speed. HOW

Three examples

She ran with great speed. HOW
He slept under the stars. WHERE
They arrived after the show ended. WHEN

Spot the adverbial phrase

The dog barked loudly in the middle of the night.
What is the verb?
Barked is the verb.
What is the adverbial phrase?
"In the middle of the night" is the adverbial phrase. (Note: "loudly" is a single adverb, not a phrase.)
What question does it answer?
When did the dog bark? In the middle of the night. WHEN

Build your own

Start with a simple sentence and add adverbial phrases to it.

The boy laughed...
The boy laughed during the movie. WHEN
The boy laughed on the bus. WHERE
The boy laughed with a loud snort. HOW
Challenge: Can you suggest a WHY phrase before it is revealed? The boy laughed because the joke caught him off guard. WHY
