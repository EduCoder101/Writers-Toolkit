Learning intention: We are learning to build the background knowledge and vocabulary we need to understand a new novel, and to innovate on a writer's sentences by changing key words while keeping the structure.

Success criteria
  • I can use clues from a book cover, images and vocabulary to make sensible predictions about a story.
  • I can explain what at least 6 new words from chapter 1 mean.
  • I can identify the key words a writer chose (subject, setting, verbs, adjectives) in a sentence.
  • I can innovate on one of Katherine Rundell's sentences by substituting key words while keeping her sentence structure.

Innovate & transform

Start simple. Build up. Then make Katherine Rundell's writing your own.

What is innovating & transforming?

You take a sentence someone else wrote and you change parts of it to make it your own. The structure stays. The words change.

We'll build up in three levels: opposites first, then small substitutions, then Katherine Rundell's actual writing. Then it's your turn.

What we changed

Subject students → monkeys. The "who" of the sentence.
Setting fun-filled holiday → stress-soaked time at the zoo. The "where" and the mood.
Action played games → kids pointed at them. The "what happened".

Step 0 of 5

What changed

Title FLIGHT → DESCENT. The opposite kind of motion.
Adjective phrase man-made magic wish → silent stone. Same simile shape, different image.
Subject aeroplane → diver. The "who or what".
Verb began to rise → plummeted. The action is now downwards and dramatic.
Setting Added "towards the ocean floor". A new location grounds the new image.

Three different writers might innovate it like this

Mood: terrifying
"Like a deep, swallowing nightmare, the submarine began to sink."
What changed: "magic wish" → "swallowing nightmare". "aeroplane" → "submarine". "rise" → "sink". The structure is identical.
Mood: joyful
"Like a brightly painted balloon, the kite began to dance."
What changed: "magic wish" → "brightly painted balloon". "aeroplane" → "kite". "rise" → "dance". Same lifting feeling, but lighter and more playful.
Mood: peaceful
"Like a long, slow breath, the hot-air balloon began to drift."
What changed: "magic wish" → "long, slow breath". "aeroplane" → "hot-air balloon". "rise" → "drift". The pace slows right down.

Self-assessment: check each of your three innovations
