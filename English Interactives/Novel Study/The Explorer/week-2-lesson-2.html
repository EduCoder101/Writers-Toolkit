Learning Intention and Success Criteria

Chapter 4: The River

Learning intention: We are learning to build vocabulary, summarise key events, and think critically about survival challenges in a text.

Success criteria
  • I can define unfamiliar vocabulary from the chapter and use context to understand meaning.
  • I can read aloud with expression and summarise the key events of a chapter in one sentence.
  • I can identify and rank the most important survival challenges the characters face.

How this works

Before typing your definition into the shared document, write it in your notebook first as a personal record. Add words to the shared document one at a time to avoid accidental deletions.

Phase 2 / 7
Phase 3 of 7 · Pairs

Re-read The River Aloud

Focus on expression and prosody as you read with a partner

Paired reading

With a partner, re-read Chapter 4 (The River). Take turns reading, alternating paragraphs. Focus on reading with expression. Think about where the tension rises, where a character's voice changes, and how punctuation guides your pacing.

Summarise the chapter

After reading, work together to identify the key events of this chapter. Remember: a summary captures what matters most, not every detail. Your teacher will remind you to focus on events that move the story forward.

Phase 3 / 7
Phase 4 of 7 · Small Groups

Survival Planning

What would you need to survive in the Amazon?

Identify the key problems

In your group, discuss the major problems the children will face. Think about: weather, animals, food and water, medicine, and clothing. What else might you add?

Create and rank your survival list

As a group, list the 5 to 10 most important things you would need to find, make, or do to survive. Then rank them in order of importance, with the most critical at the top. Be ready to justify your ranking to the class.

Phase 4 / 7
Phase 5 of 7 · Independent (if time)

Diary Entry

Imagine you are one of the children lost in the Amazon

Write a diary entry

Choose one of the four children and write a diary entry from their point of view. What are your first thoughts and fears? What do you notice about your surroundings? How do you feel about the other children you are stranded with?

Extension: Bug Restaurant

Listen to the ABC Kids Listen podcast 'Dictionaries and Bug Restaurant', starting at the 4-minute mark. As you listen, think about how the idea of a 'bug restaurant' connects to what the children in The Explorer might need to do to survive.

After listening, write one or two sentences explaining the connection.

Phase 5 / 7
Phase 6 of 7 · Whole Class

Plenary

Share, discuss, and record

Share your survival rankings

Each group shares their top 3 survival priorities and explains why they ranked them that way. Did different groups agree or disagree? What was the hardest item to rank?

Share diary entries

Volunteers read their diary entry aloud. As you listen, notice which character each writer chose and how they captured that character's voice and feelings.

Update your Character Development Record

Add any new observations from Chapter 4. What have the characters done or said in this chapter that changes or deepens your understanding of who they are?
